KIRILOV Vehicle Control Unit V3 – Powering Your 4-Wheeled Robot

Published: Oct 23, 2025 | Category: Robotics

If you’re diving into robotics and need a flexible, easy-to-use control board for a 4-wheeled robot, the KIRILOV Vehicle Control Unit V3 is built exactly for that. It’s ideal for both development and prototyping.

Overview

The control unit is powered by an ESP32 microcontroller, handling logic, communication, and motor control. Two L293D motor driver ICs interface directly with your DC motors, allowing forward, backward, and precise turning movements. A set of development-friendly side pins makes connecting sensors and modules simple.

Key Features

  • ESP32 Core: Dual-core Wi-Fi & Bluetooth microcontroller. Reads sensors, executes motor control, and communicates wirelessly.
  • Motor Control: Two L293D ICs control four DC motors (two per IC), supporting bidirectional movement and precise differential steering.
  • Development Pins: Exposed pins along the edges for sensors, actuators, logic probing, or circuit experimentation.
  • Power Management: Dedicated 3.3V, 5V, and GND pins for ESP32 and external peripherals like servos or sensors.

How It Works

  1. Input Signals: Commands come from sensors, remote controllers, or Wi-Fi/Bluetooth.
  2. ESP32 Processing: Interprets commands and decides motor actions using custom firmware.
  3. Motor Driving: ESP32 sends signals to L293D drivers, which switch higher current to motors safely.
  4. Robot Movement: Forward/backward and differential steering allow precise navigation.
  5. Debug & Expand: Side pins enable monitoring and adding new modules like distance sensors or LEDs.

Why This Board Stands Out

  • Dual Functionality: Works as a standalone motor controller or a development board for sensors and circuits.
  • Ease of Troubleshooting: Exposed signals mean no need to desolder or probe tiny pads.
  • Expandable & Flexible: Add line-following, obstacle detection, or wireless modules with minimal wiring.

Applications

  • Educational robotics kits
  • Rapid prototyping of small autonomous vehicles
  • Motor control development projects
  • IoT-based robotic applications

Final Thoughts

The KIRILOV Vehicle Control Unit V3 combines power, flexibility, and usability. With ESP32, L293D motor drivers, and accessible development pins, it’s both a learning platform and a reliable robotic control system—perfect for building fully autonomous 4-wheeled robots or experimenting with sensors and motor projects.
